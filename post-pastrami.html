The Art of the Slow-Cooked Pastrami

Why we brine for ten days, smoke for twelve hours, and never cut corners.

There's no shortcut to a great pastrami. We start with whole briskets, hand-trim them in the morning, and lower them into a brine of coriander, mustard seed, garlic, and bay. They sit for ten days.

The Smoke

After the brine comes the rub — cracked pepper and coriander, heavy-handed. We smoke over hardwood for twelve hours, low and slow, until the bark is dark and the fat has rendered to silk.

A great sandwich starts long before lunch service.

We slice to order, pile it warm on rye, and finish with mustard. That's the whole story.
